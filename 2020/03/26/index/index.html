Question: Laplacian diffusion of momentum or PV

11 May 2019 by MiniUFO

Introduction

Many variables can be used to describe the state of a fluid (e.g., atmosphere and ocean), such as momentum vector $\mathbf u$, potential temperature $\theta$, thickness $h$, potential vorticity (PV) $q$ etc. All these variables seem to be applicable to the Laplacian diffusion operator as $\nabla^2 \mathbf u$, $\nabla^2 \theta$, $\nabla^2 h$ and $\nabla^2 q$. However, some of them are not consistent with each other or some physics.

1. Examples

1.1 non-diffusive planetary vorticity $f$

Absolute $\zeta_a$ is the sum of relative vorticity $\zeta$ and planetary vorticity $f$:

$$\zeta_a=\zeta+f$$

While $\zeta$ can be diffusive, $f$ is generally not diffusive (Nakamura and Zhu 2010). As a result, $\zeta_a$ is not diffused as $\nabla^2 \zeta_a$, but rather:

$$\nabla^2 (\zeta_a- f)=\nabla^2 \zeta$$

to ensure global conservation of zonal momentum.

1.2 vorticity/thickness diffusion v.s. PV diffusion

The barotropic vorticity model is:

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial \zeta_a}{\partial t}+\nabla\cdot (\mathbf u \zeta_a)&=\kappa_m \nabla^2\zeta_a \tag{1}\
\frac{\partial h}{\partial t}+\nabla\cdot (\mathbf u h)&=0 \tag{2}\
\frac{\partial \sigma}{\partial t}+\nabla\cdot (\mathbf u \sigma)&=0 \tag{2’}
\end{align}
$$

where density $\sigma$ is the inverse of thickness $h$. Why Eq. (2) or Eq. (2’) does not have a Laplacian diffusion term $\kappa_h\nabla^2 h$ can be found at stackexchange. Given that barotropic PV is defined as $q=\zeta_a/h=\sigma\zeta$, we can have the advection-diffusion equation for PV as:

$$\frac{\partial q}{\partial t}=-\mathbf u\cdot \nabla q+\kappa_m \nabla^2q \tag{3}$$

It is not possible to verify that Eqs. (1-3) are exactly equivalent to Eq. (4). Considering the diffusion term only, we have:

$$
\frac{\partial q}{\partial t}\sim \nabla^2q
= \nabla^2 (\sigma\zeta_a)
=\sigma\nabla^2 \zeta_a+2\nabla\zeta_a\cdot\nabla \sigma+\zeta_a\nabla^2 \sigma
$$

References

  1. Nakamura, N., and D. Zhu, 2010: Finite-amplitude wave activity and diffusive flux of potential vorticity in eddy-mean flow interaction. J. Atmos. Sci., 67, 2701-2716.
